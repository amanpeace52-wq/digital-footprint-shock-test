Could a Stranger Map Your Life from One Account?

A quick, student-friendly challenge about how “trackable” you are from one social or gaming profile.

Created by Aman P Kaur · Cyber Security & Digital Identity Educator

How this challenge works

This is a mini OSINT-style exercise. You’ll reflect on one account and see how easy it might be for someone to join the dots about you.

  1. Pick one account to focus on (Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, gaming, etc.).
  2. Answer the quiz questions as honestly as possible based on that account.
  3. See your exposure rating and quick feedback.
  4. Move to Smart Moves to get 3–5 practical changes you can make this week.
Important: You will not be asked for usernames or passwords. This tool does not send your answers anywhere – it runs entirely in your browser.
  • When you’re ready, click the “Quiz” tab above or use the button below.
    • Quiz – Think about one account

    Answer based on how you usually behave, not the “perfect” answer. This should take about 3–5 minutes.

    Part 1 – Identity & visibility
    1. How close is your main username/handle to your real identity?
    2. Do you reuse this same username across platforms?
    3. Is this account public or private?
    Quick fact: Security analysts often say, “Your handle plus your posts is enough to build a surprisingly accurate profile”. This is what you’re testing right now.
    Part 2 – Content, routine & scenarios
    4. Does this profile show or hint at any of the following?
    Think about photos, bios, captions and tagged locations.
    5. If a stranger scrolls your last 12 posts, could they guess your weekly routine?
    6. How often do you post with visible locations or tags?
    Geo-tag, school sign, workplace logo, bus stop, etc.
    7. Scenario – New follow request
    Someone you barely know sends a follow / friend request. You recognise their name from school/uni but don’t know them well.
    8. Scenario – Suspicious link in DMs
    You get a DM from someone you know with a short message and a link: “OMG is this you in this video?”
    Your rating & exposure level

    This is a snapshot based on your answers about one account. It’s not a judgment – it’s a starting point.

    This rating is about how easy it would be for a stranger to build a picture of your life – not whether you are “good” or “bad” online.
    Smart moves to make this week

    Pick one or two actions that feel realistic for you. You don’t have to fix everything at once.
